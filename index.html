What is CubeFreight?

CubeFreight is a suite of three free logistics calculators built for packaging engineers, warehouse planners and LTL shippers. Each tool solves one step of the same chain: the Carton calculator designs the shipping box around your product, the Pallet calculator finds the best way to stack those boxes on a pallet, and the LTL calculator estimates the NMFC freight class that determines what the shipment costs to move.

Everything is a plain web page — dimensions never leave your browser, results render as interactive 3D views, and every tool exports a printable PDF report you can hand to the warehouse or your carrier.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are the calculators really free?

Yes. All three calculators are free, with no account, no trial limit and no watermarks on the PDF exports. The site is supported by advertising.

Which calculator should I start with?

Follow the shipping chain: if you are still deciding the box size, start with the Carton calculator — it also shows the pallet load for each candidate box. If your carton is fixed, use the Pallet calculator to maximize the load. If you ship LTL in North America, run the result through the LTL calculator to estimate the freight class.

Does my data leave the browser?

No. Dimensions, weights and quantities are processed entirely in your browser by JavaScript. Nothing you type is uploaded or stored on a server.

Which pallet standards are supported?

T11 (1100×1100, KR/JP), Euro EPAL (1200×800), US GMA (48×40 in), ISO industrial (1200×1000), Australian (1165×1165) and fully custom sizes, in mm, cm or inches.

How accurate is the freight class estimate?

The LTL calculator applies the official density break points, including the July 2025 13-sub scale, and calculates density exactly the way the NMFC measures a handling unit — pallet included. Actual classification can still differ for commodities with special stowability, handling or liability rules, so treat it as an estimate.

What will the Pro version add?

A desktop-grade suite for daily production use: mixed-SKU palletizing, double stacking, brick stacking patterns, container planning and bulk exports. It is in development — the free web calculators stay free.
